The Alberta high school diploma

The Alberta high school diploma is an official document issued by Alberta Education (http://www.education.alberta.ca) and certifies that the holder has completed a prescribed program of instruction at a high school or through other formats under the direction and supervision of Alberta Education.

To attain a high school diploma, a student must:

  • Earn a minimum of 100 credits
  • Complete, and meet the standards of, the following courses:
    • ELA 30-1 or 30-2
    • Social studies 30-1 or 30-2
    • Mathematics 20-1, 20-2, or 20-3
    • Biology, Physics, Chemistry, or Science 20, or Science 24
    • Physical education 10
    • Career and life management
  • Earn at least 10 credits, in any combination, from any of the following:
    • Career and technology studies
    • Fine arts
    • Interntional languages
    • Physical education 20/30
    • Locally developed/acquired and authorized courses.
    • Two 35 level courses from any trade in the Registered Apprenticeship Program
  • Earn at least 10 credits in any 30-level courses (in addition to any courses listed above)
